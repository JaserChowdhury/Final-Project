This single quote informs how I conduct myself in the three core aspects of my life.
Care to know how?

Personal

In my personal life, this applies abstractly. It more or less translates to me trying to be the best human I can possibly be. Kind. Helpful. Steadfast in my convictions. Fun to be around. Empathetic. Reliable. These are some of the pillars I hope and try to uphold, albeit with varying degrees of success. I would like to remembered as an upstanding citizen of the highest degree, and a big part of that is fostering a sense of community wherever I go.

School

School is relatively straightforward in this regard: With every class I take, I try to expand my knowledge in the subject matter further than the curriculum accounts for. I like to learn, which in this case means learning as much as possible as quick as possible. I try to come out of every class being an expert at the subject (in some courses this is far easier than others). This plays a bit into my course selection, which is why I tend to go for classes that are broad in scope (in a metaphorical sense, it means a bigger buffet).

Career

Work is the toughest of the 3 to excel in given my youth and subsequent inexperience, but it doesn't stop me from trying voraciously. Right now, I work as a Junior Developer at a telecommunications company. In trying to be the best employee I can be, I work extra hours and try to learn as much of the business as I can so that I stay as prepared as I can. But work isn't entirely about productivity, it's about the camaraderie you build with your coworkers. While I still have areas to improve in, I try my best to relate to my peers and be as welcoming as possible.

If I had to boil the essence of my existence down to one word, it would be self-improvement (I count hyphenated words as 1 ;). I feel like life gets dull if you stay the same person doing the same things. So... here's to hoping that my life stays interesting.
